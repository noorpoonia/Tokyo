From dazzling night views to peaceful promenades, here are some areas that are well-worth visiting, both on and off the beaten track. Trek on up to the tops of mountains and towers for sweeping panoramas, or down to valleys and alleys for more intimate scenes. For a fresh look at the city, try some of the more unique modes of transportation, such as a rickshaw tour, a rental bicycle or a river cruise.

Top Attractions in Tokyo

Central Tokyo

Tsukiji Outer Market

Market for fresh seafood and produce.

Imperial Palace

Main residence of the Imperial Family.

Koishikawa Korakuen

Landscape garden next to Tokyo Dome.

Northern Tokyo

Tokyo National Museum

One of Japan's oldest and best museums.

Tokyo Skytree

634 meter tall tower with observation decks.

Sensoji District

Large temple in the Asakusa district.

Western Tokyo

Shibuya

Popular district among younger generations.

Meiji Shrine

Dedicated to the deity of Emperor Meiji.

Shinjuku Gyoen

Spacious city park in Shinjuku.

Southern Tokyo

Institute of Nature

Quiet nature reserve in the middle of Tokyo.

Odaiba

Futuristic architecture on a man made island.

Roppongi Hills

Modern entertainment and shopping complex.
